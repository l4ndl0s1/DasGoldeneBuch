MISSION

Das Goldene Buch is a feminist initiative that provides a platform for women* who have never had the opportunity to connect with art, enabling them to tell their own stories, express themselves freely, and unlock their creative potential. As a space for creative liberation, DGB is not only an artistic production but also part of a broader movement for social solidarity and change. In addition to workshops, various events such as poetry readings, concerts, and performances bring women* together to create a collective space for production and sharing. One of the main goals of our initiative is to establish DGB as a regularly published independent publication, amplifying women*â€™s voices and reaching a wider audience.

This project aims to provide a space for migrant women*, who are often rendered invisible in the face of social, cultural, and economic challenges, to share their stories, express themselves, and connect meaningfully with other communities. In this project, migrant women* who face language barriers, gender-based restrictions, bureaucratic obstacles, legal insecurity, and economic difficulties in their daily lives are empowered by coming together in a network of solidarity and trust. The project not only offers a space for individual expression and creative freedom, but also creates bonds between women* across borders. This platform, where migrant and non-migrant women* meet in a supportive community, provides a strong foundation for building a collective sense of solidarity. Workshops, events, and creative production processes not only allow women* to explore their artistic potential but also enable women* from different backgrounds to learn from each other's experiences and be mutually inspired. This platform is not only a safe space but also a laboratory where women* discover their potential and find the courage to reshape social structures. It provides a space to make their voices heard and supports them to be part of a movement that empowers them economically, socially, and politically. Crossing borders, questioning norms, and strengthening solidarity, this feminist space invites us to sow the seeds of a more egalitarian future together.
